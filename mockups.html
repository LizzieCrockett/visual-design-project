Website mock-ups

This site is a content source which serves as online support for a schools immersive theatre project (for Key Stage 3, students aged 11-14) about the playwright Aphra Behn. My overall visual concept for the site was to be both an unmistakably digital space, but also one which conveyed elements of the world of 17th century theatre. In keeping with the visual style we associate with Restoration-era England, I wanted the space to convey visual cues of sensuousness and extravagance. Achieving a strong sense of place also helps back up the immersive theatre work the site is supporting. However, I also wanted to keep the space clean and without unnecessary embellishment. This requires a certain amount of compromise, design-wise. I used the Lely portrait as a background image because I felt this image provided just that sense of sumptuousness, while still being a single detail and not overcomplicating the space. The mockups were created in Photoshop using artboards on the Web Most Common preset (1366x768). I was delighted to find the ideal typeface to convey the historical period in IM Fell Double Pica (with IM Fell Double Pica SC for uppercase text). It is a direct replica of a 1680s typeface. When selecting a typeface for educational purposes, readability was if anything even more important than it would be normally. There are a number of variants of IM Fell, but I felt Double Pica was the one which conveyed the best readability due to the greatest regularity between the letters.

Homepage

Download .psd file

The homepage of the site is the main display for the portrait of Aphra Behn which serves as a background for the entire site. The Acts are menu links. The layout is inspired by 17th century playbills (with compromises for readability and accessibility). I wanted to include a hint of the quirks of printing at this time - the mixing of uppercase, capitalisation and sentence case. This layout and choice of typeface again aims to convey a strong sense of place and time.

Times page

Download .psd file

This page is a static source of biographical content, and displays modular boxes with 16:9 images and around 125 words of text. I decided on this layout after doing heuristic evaluations of other educational sites aimed at Key Stage 3. I feel it breaks the information down into easily digestible chunks. I chose the typeface ABeeZee as the best solution for readability, bearing in mind the educational content and the wide range of abilities catered for in this content. This typeface is specifically designed with readability for children in mind.
